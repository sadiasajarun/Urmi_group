# Domain Research — Urmi Group Marketing Homepage

**Generated:** 2026-05-07 by `/fullstack-pm urmi --phase P3-design`
**Scope:** Homepage-only — 3 visual variations for client review

---

## 1. The Brief in One Sentence

A B2B portfolio homepage for Urmi Group (Bangladesh's first Full-Green RMG manufacturer) that distinctly represents Urmi as a conscious, sustainable manufacturer — not generic SaaS, not corporate-tech, not fast-fashion.

The previous demo was rejected as "generic." The redesign anchors on:
- The Bengali word **ঊর্মি = "wave"** (continuity, river heritage, never-stops innovation)
- A **blue-driven palette** (replacing the original earth-tone direction) sampled from the actual logo
- Sustainability as the **operating system**, not a section

## 3. Anti-References (Visual Patterns to Avoid)

- Generic SaaS landing pages with gradient hero blobs
- AI-startup landing pages (purple/teal gradients, glassmorphism, particle effects)
- Fast-fashion e-commerce (red sale tags, urgency banners, "Add to Cart" affordances)
- Auto-cycling hero carousels with arrows and dots
- Stock factory photography (cold blue-grey, no human warmth)
- "Earth-in-hands" / "lightbulb-with-leaves" sustainability cliché imagery
- Saturated greens (lime, mint, tech-eco)
- Pure white or pure black at any scale

## 5. Brand Constraints (Locked from Knowledge Base)

### Voice
- Confident, restrained, poetic
- Short sentences
- The Bengali phrase OR a wave/river/thread metaphor must appear in every section
- **Banned vocabulary:** "elevate," "harness," "innovative solutions," "in today's world," "leverage," "synergy"
- **Banned punctuation:** exclamation marks (anywhere)

### Locked Numbers (must appear, must match)
- 44+ tons fabric daily
- 250,000+ garments daily
- 4,000+ m³ wastewater purified daily (zero discharge)
- 2030 decarbonization target
- 16,800 colleagues
- 30+ global brands
- 40+ years of craft

### Locked Section Structure (per the as-built homepage)
1. Hero (with the wave metaphor)
2. Pillars (4 cards: years / hands / green / brands)
3. By the Numbers (the 4 stats above)
4. Crafts (6: Garments / Textile / Printing / Shipping / Embroidery / Washing)
5. Sustainability (5 commitments: ETP / Full Green / Decarbonization / Circular Water / Audit)
6. Products (6 categories: Active / Evening / Night / Seamless / Knitwear / Kids)
7. IMPACT values (I·M·P·A·C·T)
8. Factories (FTML / UGL / UHM on a map)
9. Clients (12+ logos with names)
10. Awards (8 visuals)
11. CTA (3 buttons: Buyer / Team / Factory)
12. Footer

The 3 variations differ in **visual language**, not section count or content.

## 7. Key Tradeoffs

- **Authenticity vs polish** — too polished risks reading "generic SaaS"; too raw risks reading "amateur."
- **Wave metaphor density** — the Bengali wordmark + ripple language must land in every section, but overplaying ("wave wave wave") becomes a parody. v2 user feedback explicitly asked to dial it back from the river-river-river framing.
- **Sustainability language** — every claim must be backed by a number or a third-party auditor. Vague "eco" copy reads as greenwashing.
- **Brand-blue dominance vs sustainability-green presence** — sustainability section needs visual differentiation (sage), but blue must remain the dominant identity.
