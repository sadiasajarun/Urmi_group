Every river begins as a stone in still water.

In 1955, on the slow brown bend of the Buriganga, a single brickfield began turning earth into shelter. From that one kiln came a navigation company, then a shipping line that carried petroleum up the Padma and the Meghna for half a century, and — in 1984 — a small garments unit named for a Bengali word that had quietly carried a family for three generations.

ঊর্মি — the wave. Four decades later, that single garments unit has become one of Bangladesh's largest readymade garment groups. A hundred and fifty production lines. Three hundred and fifteen thousand garments leaving our floors every single day. Sixteen thousand eight hundred and ninety-three people — in Demra, in Tejgaon, in Sreepur, in Siddhirganj — turning cotton into the clothes worn in Europe, Japan, North America, and beyond.

Three factories run on closed-loop water. The country's first full-green certification. Rooftop solar generating 3,145 MWh every year. Twenty-two new companies founded between 1955 and 2022, the last one bearing the name that says everything about who we are: Wave Riders Limited.

And every morning, the river rises, and the wave begins again.

Building the future of Conscious Manufacturing.

Our Mission

A current with a conscience.

Human Rights

Urmi has an exceptional history of worker safety, wellbeing, and inclusivity — because excellence isn't sustainable if the people making it aren't safe, heard, and well. Every factory carries an on-site medical centre, a child care facility, clean water, and climate-controlled floors. 89 differently-abled colleagues are employed across our group. 48,124 hours of training delivered in 2023–24 alone.

Design + Innovation

A dedicated in-house studio contributes fresh ideas and assists buyers in optimising seasonal collections. When your brief arrives, a design team is already inside it.

Eco Obsession

Sustainable factory planning and production processes built the country's first full Green Factory. An ETP processing 4,000 m³ of wastewater daily. 64 tons of fabric waste recycled. A Decarbonisation roadmap running from 2008 to 2027. Not a policy document — a factory floor decision.

Digital Transformation

Urmi is reimagining ways of working more efficiently through automation and digital solutions — while consistently exceeding customer expectations. An RFID Journal Award in 2022 and a Digital Bangladesh Award in the same year confirm the direction we're moving.

One unbroken wave.

Hover over any year. The wave has been building since before you were born.

1950s
1955 Fatulla Brick Field

The origin. A brickfield on the Buriganga River, turning clay into building material. The group that would one day dress the world began with earth and fire.

1958 Stoneville Engineering

The second stone. Engineering capabilities built from the ground up alongside the brick trade.

1974 URMI Navigation

The river becomes a highway. Urmi begins chartering the waterways of Bangladesh — the Padma, the Meghna — decades before most competitors even knew them by name.

1979 URMI Shipping

A formal shipping arm is established, carrying petroleum for Bangladesh Petroleum Corporation. The wave learns to move across water.

1984 URMI Garments

ঊর্মি is born. A small garments unit in Demra, Dhaka. The name means wave. It was, from the beginning, a promise of continuous motion.

1994 Attires Manufacturing

Capacity grows. A second garments unit opens in Tejgaon, Dhaka, adding scale and specialisation.

2002 FTML Textiles

Vertical integration begins. Fakhruddin Textile Mills opens in Sreepur, Gazipur — bringing knitting, dyeing, and finishing in-house for the first time.

2006 FTML Stitching

The textile and garments arms are joined. A vertically integrated supply chain now takes cotton from fibre to finished garment under one ownership.

2008 Dots & Marks

A dedicated printing facility opens. 188,000 pieces a day, from oval printing to PAD machines to heat transfer. Every colour, every technique, in-house.

2012 Poly Corr Packaging

The chain extends to the box. Packaging is brought in-house, completing the fibre-to-shipment story.

2015 UHM — Full Green

Bangladesh's first complete green factory. A joint venture with Toray, Japan. LEED Platinum certified. The wave plants its flag on sustainable manufacturing.

2016 Seamless Knitting

Bangladesh's first and largest seamless knitting line. 78 SANTONI Top2V machines. 14,500 pieces per day of garments without a single seam to cut or waste.

2019 AgroSAL Ltd

Diversification into agriculture — a quiet nod to the land that feeds the cotton fields the group was founded on.

2021 UGL Expansion

The flagship garments unit relocates and expands capacity — now 3,228 people, 150 production lines, 315,000 pieces a day.

2022 Wave Riders Ltd

The newest company in the group. The name says everything. The wave rides on.

A workplace built on warmth.

Every factory floor at Urmi has four things that are non-negotiable: an on-site medical centre, a child care facility, clean water, and a climate-controlled working environment. These aren't CSR initiatives. They're operating standards. The logic is simple: the wave only moves as well as the people carrying it.

In 2023–24, Urmi provided 48,124 hours of training across all units. 89 differently-abled colleagues work with us today. 6.6% of our management team are women — a number we are publicly committed to raising. Our factories are audited by BetterWork Bangladesh, Amfori BSCI, the Fair Wear Foundation, and the Social & Labor Convergence Programme. The audits confirm what our workers already know.
